Why did the 1. Question happen and what caused it
   Why: AI research is theoretical, but AI patents are often about Applications (Facial recognition, autonomous driving, logistics).

   The Cause: China’s massive "Data Reservoir" (from its huge population and digitized economy) made it easier to turn a 
   theoretical paper into a patentable, real-world tool. Basically China reached critical knowledge point and needed applications more
   that theory.

   How: In 2017, the Chinese State Council issued the "New Generation Artificial Intelligence Development Plan" (AIDP).

   The Cause: This was an explicit government mandate to become the world leader in AI by 2030.

   A drop in 2019 is also apparent. That looks like its caused by covid-19 lockdown but actually,
   China’s new issue to stop junk patenting to get government rewards and shift in strategic focus caused it.

Why is the R&D Expenditure in 3. Question the biggest factor in AI growth.
   A $1 billion private investment might help one company grow. A 1% shift in GDP toward R&D funds 500 different laboratories.
   This budget pays for PhD programs and university research centers. These institutions produce the engineers that startups eventually hire.
   When the government commits a higher percentage of GDP to R&D, it acts as a "green light" 
   for the entire economy. It reduces the perceived risk for everyone else.
